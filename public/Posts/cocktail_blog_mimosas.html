Mimosas

I have never met a person who didn't absolutely love mimosas. Or, well, I suppose it's possible that I have met such a monster, but I must have immediately removed them from my life and blocked them from my memory. Either way, everyone loves mimosas!

While I would never, ever, turn down a standard orange juice mimosa, why not mix things up a little? A good, dry, sparkling wine is basically a blank canvas, which means the flavor combinations are potentially endless. Substitute any fruit juice in place of the orange juice, and kick up the flavor (and booze) quotient with a splash of fruit liqueur. Let your imagination run wild!

I typically use a ratio of 1 part juice to 3 parts sparkling wine, but feel free to adjust the amounts to your personal preferences.

If you need a little inspiration, here's a few ideas:

Pomegranate Mimosa

  • 1 part fresh pressed pomegranate juice
  • Splash of pomegranate liqueur
  • 3 parts sparkling wine
  • Garnish with pomegranate kernels

Clementine Mimosa

  • 1 part clementine juice
  • 3 parts sparkling wine
  • Garnish with clementine wedge

Blueberry Mimosa

  • 1 part blueberry liqueur
  • 3 parts sparkling wine
  • Garnish with fresh blueberries

Blackberry Mimosa

  • 1 part fresh pressed blackberry juice
  • Splash blackberry liqueur
  • 3 parts sparkling wine
  • Garnish with fresh blackberries

Cranberry Mimosa

  • 1 part unsweetened cranberry juice
  • 3 parts sparkling wine
  • Garnish with fresh cranberries

Amaretto Sour Mimosa

  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon fresh orange juice
  • 1 tablespoon amaretto liqueur
  • 4 oz sparkling wine
  • Garnish with orange slice and cherry

If you're planning a party, or serving mimosas to a group, one 750ml bottle of sparkling wine will make 6-8 drinks. So get at least two bottles, because everyone loves mimosas!
